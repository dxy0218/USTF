## Summary

The World Update Cycle is the standard procedure for advancing the world after meaningful actions or time jumps. It ensures that non-player actors continue to act, consequences persist, and open pressures evolve.

---

## 1. When To Use

Use this procedure when:

- A major player action resolves.
- A scene ends with meaningful consequences.
- Time advances by days, weeks, months, or years.
- A faction or organization has had time to act.
- A campaign resumes after a gap.
- The world state feels stale or over-centered on the player.

---

## 2. Procedure

```text
1. Confirm current in-world time.
2. Identify elapsed time.
3. Review open threads.
4. Review active actors.
5. Review active projects.
6. Review consequences currently in effect.
7. Determine which actors had opportunity to act.
8. Resolve actor actions by logic, procedure, or uncertainty.
9. Update world state.
10. Update knowledge state.
11. Update resources.
12. Add, advance, or close process trackers.
13. Identify new risks and opportunities.
14. Record changes in Session Delta or state file.
```

---

## 3. Actor Opportunity Check

For each relevant Actor, ask:

```text
Did this Actor have time to act?
Did it have resources?
Did it have motive?
Did it have necessary knowledge?
Was it constrained or blocked?
Was opposition present?
Would action be rational under its decision logic?
```

If yes, advance or resolve the Actor's action.

If no, record why no movement occurred only if important.

---

## 4. Time Scale Guidance

### Scene Time

Use for immediate consequences.

### Operational Time

Use for days or weeks of activity.

### Strategic Time

Use for months or campaign arcs.

### Historical Time

Use for years or generational change.

The longer the time jump, the more non-player actors should be considered.

---

## 5. Avoiding Over-Simulation

Do not simulate every entity.

Prioritize actors with:

- Direct relevance to current open threads
- High power
- High proximity
- High motivation
- Active plans
- Strong opposition to player interests
- Pending consequences

USTF simulates meaningful causality, not every mundane event.

---

## 6. Output

A World Update SHOULD produce:

```text
Time Advanced:
World State Changes:
Actor Changes:
Organization Changes:
Knowledge Changes:
Resource Changes:
Open Threads Advanced:
New Risks:
New Opportunities:
Continuity Issues:
```

---

## 7. Procedure Status

This is a reference procedure. Campaigns may adapt it, but adaptations must preserve Core invariants.
