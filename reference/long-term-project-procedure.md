## Summary

Long-Term Projects track efforts that require sustained time, resources, capability, and changing conditions. A project tracker records progress; it does not create causality by itself.

---

## 1. Project Definition

A Long-Term Project is an intended World State change that cannot be resolved instantly.

Examples:

- Build a company office
- Train a military unit
- Infiltrate a ministry
- Research a technology
- Repair infrastructure
- Establish trade routes
- Conduct a legal restructuring
- Build political legitimacy
- Decode an occult manuscript

## 3. Procedure

```text
1. Define desired state change.
2. Identify project owner.
3. Identify requirements.
4. Identify time scale.
5. Identify opposition and constraints.
6. Create milestones.
7. Allocate resources.
8. Advance project when time passes.
9. Check for interruptions.
10. Apply milestone consequences.
11. Complete, stall, escalate, or fail.
12. Record state changes.
```

---

## 4. Tracker Use

A tracker MAY be used to display progress.

The tracker MUST NOT be treated as the cause of success.

Progress depends on:

- Time invested
- Resources allocated
- Actor capability
- Access
- Knowledge
- Opposition
- Environmental conditions
- Interruptions

---

```text
Completed
Failed

```

---

## 6. Interruption

A project may be interrupted by:

- Resource loss
- Actor death or removal
- Exposure
- Legal action
- Military action
- Political shift
- Market shift
- Knowledge leak
- Internal conflict
- Logistical failure

Interruptions should update World State, not merely reduce a number.
