## Summary

Investigation in USTF is the process of acquiring, interpreting, validating, and acting on evidence left by World State events. It is not a plot key mechanism. Failure to discover or correctly interpret evidence does not pause the world.

---

## 1. Procedure

```text
1. Define the question being investigated.
2. Identify known facts.
3. Identify unknowns.
4. Identify possible evidence sources.
5. Check investigator access.
6. Check investigator capability.
7. Check time and resources.
8. Check opposition or concealment.
9. Resolve discovery.
10. Assign reliability.
11. Update Knowledge State.
12. Determine interpretation.
13. Apply consequences of action or inaction.
```

---

## 2. Investigation Question

Every investigation SHOULD begin with a clear question.

Examples:

```text
Who attacked the convoy?
Is the mining company financially reliable?
Where did the leak originate?
What faction controls the bishop?
What caused the anomaly?
```

---

## 3. Evidence Sources

Evidence may come from:

- Physical traces
- Documents
- Witnesses
- Records
- Financial data
- Communications
- Rumors
- Forensics
- Intelligence assets
- Surveillance
- Archaeology
- Technical scans
- Magical or supernatural means, if module allows

Evidence exists only if the world state plausibly produced it.

---

## 4. Reliability

Evidence SHOULD receive a reliability state:

```text
Confirmed
Likely
Unverified
Ambiguous
Contradictory
False
Fabricated
Contaminated
Unknown
```

Reliability is separate from interpretation.

A true clue may be misread.

A false clue may be believed.

---

## 5. Opposition

Investigations may be opposed by:

- Concealment
- Destruction of evidence
- Misinformation
- Legal barriers
- Social pressure
- Distance
- Time decay
- Corruption
- Memory failure
- Fear
- Counterintelligence

Opposition must be grounded in actor capability and knowledge.

---

## 6. Result Types

Investigation may produce:

```text
Fact discovered
Partial fact discovered
Evidence found but not understood
False lead accepted
Contradiction discovered
Source compromised
Investigator exposed
Target alerted
Time lost
Resource spent
```

---

## 7. Knowledge Update

Every meaningful discovery MUST update Knowledge State.

Record:

```text
Holder
Content
Source
Time Acquired
Reliability
Confidence
Transferability
Who does not know
```

---

## 8. World Continues

If investigation fails, the world continues.

The target does not freeze.

The mystery does not wait.

Consequences arise from delay, misunderstanding, exposure, or inaction.
