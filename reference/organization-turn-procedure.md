## Summary

The Organization Turn Procedure advances organizations as active Actors. It supports companies, states, militaries, secret societies, noble houses, criminal groups, research institutions, and other structured entities.

---

## 1. When To Use

Use when:

- An organization has time to act.
- A campaign enters operational or strategic time.
- A faction is pursuing a project.
- An organization reacts to player action.
- Internal politics or resource pressure matters.

---

## 2. Procedure

```text
1. Identify organization.
2. Review goals.
3. Review known information.
4. Review current resources.
5. Review active projects.
6. Review constraints.
7. Review internal factions.
8. Determine likely decision.
9. Check opposition and uncertainty.
10. Apply action result.
11. Update resources and exposure.
12. Update relationships.
13. Update open threads.
14. Record state change.
```

---

## 3. Organization Decision Logic

An organization SHOULD act according to:

```text
Goals
Leadership preference
Institutional culture
Resource position
Risk tolerance
Knowledge state
Political constraints
Legal constraints
Internal faction pressure
External threats
```

Organizations MUST NOT act on information they do not possess.

## 5. Internal Friction

Large organizations SHOULD NOT behave like perfectly unified individuals.

Consider:

- Bureaucracy
- Rival departments
- Leadership succession
- Corruption
- Ideological splits
- Budget fights
- Slow communication
- Legal review
- Operational inertia

Internal friction may delay, distort, or block an intended action.
