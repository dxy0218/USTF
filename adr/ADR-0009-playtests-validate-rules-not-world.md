# ADR-0009 — Playtests Validate Rules, Not Worlds

**Status:** Accepted  
**Date:** Draft  
**Related Files:** `core/17-playtest-versioning-protocol.md`

## Context

USTF campaigns such as corporate, medieval, military, horror, political, and space campaigns may reveal rule pressure. A common failure mode in long-form campaigns is to rewrite world facts to hide weak rules.

## Decision

Campaigns are treated as playtests of the engine. When a rule fails, the rule may be revised through versioning. The campaign world should not be rewritten merely to protect a flawed rule.

## Consequences

- Campaign continuity remains credible.
- Rule improvement becomes evidence-driven.
- Playtest reports become first-class artifacts.
- Rule changes require documentation and compatibility notes.
