# ADR-0006 — Investigation as World Evidence

**Status:** Accepted  
**Date:** 2026-07-01  
**Version:** v3.0.0-draft  
**Related Files:** `core/12-investigation-layer.md`, `core/07-knowledge-information-system.md`

## Context

Traditional mystery systems often treat investigation as a path through prepared clues. This creates fragility: if a clue is missed, the scenario stalls; if a clue must be found, player action may become meaningless.

USTF needs investigation to support long-form simulation across crime, intelligence, business diligence, politics, occult horror, and historical research.

## Decision

USTF treats investigation as an attempt to update Knowledge State through interaction with Evidence generated by Objective World State.

The model separates:

```text
Core Fact
Evidence
Interpretation
Knowledge State
```

No clue is a mandatory plot key by Core rule.

## Consequences

- Facts may remain undiscovered.
- Evidence may be found but misunderstood.
- False interpretations may create real consequences.
- Investigation failure does not stop world progression.
- Hidden Actors may continue acting while investigation proceeds.
- Case Files become active state for unresolved investigations.

## Compatibility Impact

This decision requires all investigation modules to obey Knowledge State and Evidence State separation.

Genre-specific investigation tools may extend methods but may not bypass this model.
